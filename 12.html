<h2>Cost Structure Comparison and Profit Decline Analysis (Rounds 3-5)</h2>


<p>The following analysis will compare the cost structures of Rounds 3, 4, and 5 to identify the primary cost drivers that contributed to the decline in profit.</p>


<h3>Problem Restatement</h3>

<p>The user wants to understand the changes in cost structures between Round 3, Round 4, and Round 5 and pinpoint the top three cost drivers responsible for the observed profit decline.</p>


<h3>Rule/Formula Citation</h3>

<p>To analyze the cost structures and profit decline, we will examine the unit cost details provided in the "COST ANALYSIS" section for each market and the overall average. Specifically, we will focus on:</p>

<ul>

    <li><strong>业务单位成本 (Business Unit Cost)</strong>: Represents the direct costs associated with producing each unit.</li>

    <li><strong>营业费用单位成本 (Operating Expense Unit Cost)</strong>: Includes marketing and freight costs per unit.</li>

    <li><strong>管理及财务费用单位成本 (Management and Financial Expense Unit Cost)</strong>: Covers R&D, raw material holding costs, administrative expenses, and interest expenses per unit.</li>

    <li><strong>总单位成本 (Total Unit Cost)</strong>: The sum of all unit costs.</li>

    <li><strong>税前净利 EBT (%) (Earnings Before Tax Margin)</strong>: Indicates the profitability per unit of sales.</li>

</ul>

<p><strong>Observations:</strong></p>

<ol>

    <li><strong>Profit Decline (R3 to R4):</strong> The EBT margin decreased from -1.95% in Round 3 to -2.55% in Round 4. This decline occurred despite a slight increase in average sales price.

    <ul>

        <li><strong>Business Unit Cost</strong> increased by $0.13.</li>

        <li><strong>Management and Financial Expense Unit Cost</strong> increased by $0.07.</li>

        <li><strong>Operating Expense Unit Cost</strong> decreased by $0.03, which was not enough to offset the other increases.</li>

        <li>The total unit cost increased by $0.17, which consumed the entire sales price increase and more, leading to a deeper loss.</li>

    </ul>

    </li>

    <li><strong>Profit Improvement (R4 to R5):</strong> The EBT margin improved significantly from -2.55% in Round 4 to -0.31% in Round 5.

    <ul>

        <li><strong>Business Unit Cost</strong> continued to increase by $0.19.</li>

        <li><strong>Management and Financial Expense Unit Cost</strong> showed a small increase of $0.02.</li>

        <li><strong>Operating Expense Unit Cost</strong> saw a substantial decrease of $0.17.</li>

        <li>The average sales price increased by $0.18, and the large reduction in operating expenses helped to nearly offset the rising business unit costs, leading to a much smaller overall loss.</li>

    </ul>

    </li>

</ol>
